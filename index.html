Faster Drug Discovery: Zebrafish Behavioral Genetics Accelerating the Next Generation of CNS Therapies

3Z Pharmaceuticals is an Icelandic drug discovery company that emerged from the Department of Technology and Engineering at Reykjavík University — founded by experts in behavioral neuroscience and neuroscience with a single mission: commercialize a zebrafish-based CNS screening platform powerful enough to discover next-generation CNS drugs faster, cheaper, and with higher translational confidence than any existing approach.

The Zebrafish Advantage for CNS Screening: Zebrafish represent the optimal intersection of human relevance, cost, and throughput for CNS drug discovery.

ADHD Non-Stimulant Pipeline — From Fish to Human Genetics: Using the adgrl3.1 genetically engineered zebrafish ADHD model — one of the most validated genetic models of ADHD in the field — 3Z identified novel non-stimulant therapeutics that rescue the hyperactive ADHD phenotype, confirmed across mammalian models.

Insomnia + Metabolic Syndrome: A Novel Mechanism: 3Z's insomnia therapeutic represents a novel mechanism of action addressing not only insomnia but also metabolic syndrome — two conditions with well-documented high comorbidity that current sleep medications entirely fail to address simultaneously.

AI Multiomics: Closing the Translation Gap: The partnership between 3Z and biotx.ai represents the state of the art in translating animal model drug discoveries into clinically validated human evidence without conducting costly Phase II trials first.

ADHD Non-Stimulant Program

3Z's lead ADHD therapeutic candidates are all non-stimulants — identified through unbiased high-throughput behavioral screening in the adgrl3.1 zebrafish ADHD model, confirmed in mammalian models, and validated in human genetic populations using AI-powered Mendelian randomization.

Insomnia + Metabolic Syndrome Program

3Z's insomnia therapeutic encompasses a novel mechanism of action discovered through zebrafish behavioral screening — targeting sleep pathways while simultaneously addressing metabolic syndrome, a condition highly comorbid with insomnia affecting hundreds of millions globally.

Zebrafish Screening Platform Services

3Z offers its unique behavior-based high-throughput zebrafish screening platform as a service to pharmaceutical companies seeking cost-effective CNS compound screening, drug repurposing candidate identification, and neurological indication assessment.

Why Choose 3Z Pharmaceuticals

The benefits of partnering with us

The Only Behavior-Based Zebrafish CNS Screening Platform Built for Drug Repurposing

Every major CNS drug discovery approach faces the same fundamental problem: rodent behavioral models are too slow and expensive for compound screening, while cell-based assays lack whole-organism CNS relevance.

Human Genetic Validation Before First Clinical Dollar Spent

The most expensive failure in CNS drug development is discovering after Phase II that an animal model finding does not translate to humans. 3Z's biotx.ai partnership directly addresses this failure mode — using UK Biobank, Estonian Biobank, Polygenic Risk Scores, and Mendelian randomization to validate CNS drug candidates in real human genetic and clinical outcome data before IND filing.

Non-Stimulant ADHD Fills a $3B+ Unmet Commercial Need

ADHD is one of the most prevalent neurodevelopmental conditions globally — yet first-line stimulant medications (methylphenidate, amphetamines) have 30%+ non-responder rates, significant abuse liability, cardiovascular side effects in at-risk populations, and regulatory restrictions limiting use in adults and comorbid cardiac patients.

Drug Repurposing Compresses Development Timeline and Investment Risk

Developing a new chemical entity from discovery to approval costs $1-2 billion and takes 10-15 years on average — with an 88% failure rate. 3Z's repurposing strategy exploits the regulatory shortcut: when approval refers to existing publications and originator approval data, development timelines compress by approximately 3 years and investment requirements shrink dramatically.
